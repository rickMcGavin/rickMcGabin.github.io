About Bobby Fischer

Robert James "Bobby" Fischer (March 9, 1943 – January 17, 2008) was an American chess Grandmaster and the eleventh World Chess Champion. Many consider him the greatest chess player of all time

  • Born in Michael Reese Hospital in Chicago, Illinois
  • Born to mother Regina Fischer, who was a U.S. Citizen
  • Born to father Hans-Gerhardt Fischer, a German biphysicist

In 1956, Fischer experienced a "meteoric rise" in his playing strength. On the tenth national rating list of the United States Chess Federation (USCF), published on May 20, 1956, Fischer's rating was 1726, more than 900 points below top-rated Samuel Reshevsky (2663).

  • Won "The Game of the Century" at age 13
  • Played in 8 U.S. Championships by age 14.
  • Became the youngest grandmaster at age 15.

In 1970, Fischer began a new effort to become World Champion. His dramatic march toward the title made him a household name and made chess front-page news. He won the title in 1972, but forfeited it three years later.

Brian carney stated, "The world championship he won ... validated his view of himself as a chess player, but it also insulated him from the humanizing influences of the world around him. He descended into ... madness"
